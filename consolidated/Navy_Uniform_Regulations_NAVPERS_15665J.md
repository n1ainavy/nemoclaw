## Chapter 1: General Uniform Regulations

### Section 1: General Information

#### Article 1101: Policy

The Navy Uniform Regulations establish standardized requirements for all personnel regarding the proper wear and maintenance of naval uniforms. These regulations apply to all active duty, reserve, and other military personnel within the Department of the Navy.

**Purpose:**
- Maintain a professional military appearance
- Ensure uniformity and identification of naval personnel
- Project a professional image of naval readiness and discipline
- Establish clear standards for all components of naval uniforms

**Scope of Application:**
- All active duty sailors
- All reserve sailors
- All Naval Reserve Officers
- All warrant officers
- All civilians wearing Navy uniforms in official capacity

#### Article 1101.1: Uniform Board Recommendations (UPDATED July 2025)

The Navy Uniform Board continuously reviews Navy uniform matters and provides recommendations for policy updates. The Board's composition includes representatives from:
- Chief of Naval Operations
- Bureau of Naval Personnel
- Naval Personnel Command
- Fleet commanders
- Naval Reserve Force
- Chief of Naval Education and Training

Policy changes are implemented through NAVADMIN messages or official Navy directives. The Board balances appearance standards, functionality, cost-effectiveness, and sailor input when making recommendations.

### Section 2: Uniform Standards

#### Article 1102: Professional Military Appearance (PMA)

All military personnel must maintain professional appearance standards while in uniform at all times.

**General Requirements:**
- Uniforms must be clean, in good repair, and properly fitted
- No unauthorized modifications to uniform components
- All insignia and devices must be properly positioned and secured
- Uniform must present a military bearing and professional image

**Fitness Standards:**
- Uniforms must fit properly (not overly tight or excessively loose)
- Alterations authorized to achieve proper fit
- Tailor-made or altered uniforms must maintain uniform appearance
- Personalization beyond official markings not authorized

### Section 3: Uniform Components and Wear

#### Article 1103: Occasion for Wear

Each uniform type has specific authorized occasions for wear. Commands may impose more restrictive wear policies but may not relax Navy-wide standards.

**Uniform Change Dates:**
- Winter uniforms: October 1 - April 30 (by region/command)
- Summer uniforms: May 1 - September 30 (by region/command)
- Year-round uniforms: Navy Working Uniforms (NWU), Physical Training Uniform (PTU)
- Commanding officers establish specific wear occasions for their commands

**Seasonal Considerations:**
- Region and climate may affect uniform wear schedules
- Temperature thresholds guide uniform transitions
- Commands in extreme climates may have modified schedules

#### Article 1104: Uniform Components and Proper Fit

**Composition Requirements:**
- Uniforms consist of prescribed components worn together
- Each component serves a specific function in the complete uniform
- Improper substitution of components not authorized
- Incomplete uniforms not appropriate for wear

**Fit Standards:**
- Jacket: Should hang naturally from shoulders; sleeves end at wrist bone
- Trousers/Skirts: Proper waist and length; no excess bunching or dragging
- Shirts/Blouses: Proper fit through chest and arms; appropriate collar positioning
- Footwear: Shoes properly fitted; no excessive wear or damage

#### Article 1105: Uniform Ownership and Accountability

**Personnel Responsibility:**
- Sailors are responsible for proper maintenance of assigned uniforms
- Uniforms must be stored properly to prevent damage
- Regular cleaning and care extends uniform service life
- Damage from negligence may result in replacement cost to individual

**Reporting Requirements:**
- Report loss or damage through chain of command
- Document circumstances of uniform loss or damage
- Retain uniform items until officially released
- Turnover uniforms upon separation or transfer

### Section 4: Uniform Allowances and Replacement

#### Article 1106: Initial Uniform Issue

**Upon Accession:**
- Recruit Training Command (RTC) issues initial uniform package
- Officer Candidates issue uniforms during training
- Complete uniform set sufficient for 90-day deployment
- All components issued at government expense

**Components Included:**
- All required service dress items (blue and white uniforms)
- Working uniform (NWU Type I, II, or III as assigned)
- Physical Training Uniform (PTU)
- All required insignia and name tapes
- Undergarments and socks/hosiery

#### Article 1107: Annual Uniform Allowance

**Allowance Amounts:**
- Enlisted (E1-E6): Prescribed annual allowance
- Enlisted (E7-E9): Prescribed annual allowance
- Officers (O1-O6): Prescribed annual allowance
- Officers (O7-O10): Prescribed annual allowance

**Allowance Adjustments:**
- Increases with promotion
- Adjustments for uniform cost changes
- Additional allowance for maternity uniforms (pregnant sailors)
- Special allowance for flight personnel (flight suits)

**Use of Allowance:**
- Covers replacement of uniforms worn in normal service
- Covers alterations and repairs
- Does not cover damage from negligence
- Annual allowance resets; unused balance does not carry forward

#### Article 1108: Replacement Uniform Policy

**Wear and Tear Replacement:**
- Uniforms destroyed in normal wear replaced at no cost to sailor
- Tears, fading, color change from proper maintenance covered
- Zipper failures and normal deterioration covered
- Documentation through command administration

**Non-Covered Damage:**
- Intentional damage or abuse
- Negligent damage beyond normal wear
- Damage from unauthorized modifications
- Improper storage or care causing mildew or deterioration

**Transition Upon Advancement:**
- Sailors advanced in rank issued new uniform components for new rank
- Prior uniform rank insignia removed; replaced with new rank
- Allowance adjusted for promotion; supplemental issue may be provided
- Special issue (e.g., CPO dress) upon advancement to E7

#### Article 1109: Uniform Allowance Schedule (FY2024 - Updated March 27, 2024)

**Current Schedule:**
- Details current annual and initial allowance amounts
- Reflects most recent approved uniform costs
- Subject to change based on appropriations and inflation
- Refer to official MyNavy HR website for current values

### Section 5: Uniform Standards Enforcement

#### Article 1110: Command Responsibility

**Commanding Officers:**
- Ensure all subordinates comply with Navy uniform standards
- May impose more stringent standards within their command
- Enforce grooming and appearance standards
- Address uniform violations through appropriate channels

**Supply and Uniform Issue:**
- Ensure uniform distribution and accountability
- Monitor uniform condition and serviceability
- Facilitate uniform replacement through proper procedures
- Track uniform allowance usage

#### Article 1111: Training and Education

**Initial Military Training:**
- Recruit Training Command (RTC) provides uniform instruction
- Officer Candidate and accession programs include uniform standards
- Sailor's Creed and professional bearing emphasized
- Grooming and appearance standards enforced from day one

**Continuing Education:**
- Petty Officers and Senior Enlisted Leaders responsible for training subordinates
- Command Master Chief ensures uniform compliance
- New uniform changes communicated via NAVADMIN
- Summary of Changes documents distributed to commands

### Section 6: Transitional Provisions and Special Circumstances

#### Article 1112: Maternity and Nursing Mother Accommodations

**Maternity Uniforms:**
- Specially designed uniforms for pregnant sailors
- Service Dress Blue maternity uniform available
- Maternity-modified components accommodate physical changes
- Nursing mother accommodations (lactation rooms) supported

**Uniform Modifications:**
- Standard uniforms may be temporarily altered for pregnancy
- Return to standard uniforms authorized post-pregnancy
- No penalty for uniform transitions
- Medical provider guidance followed

#### Article 1113: Retiree Uniform Wear

**Authorization:**
- Retired personnel authorized to wear Navy uniform
- Grooming standards apply when in uniform
- Retired rank insignia worn on appropriate components
- Retirees accord themselves and the Navy professional image

**Restrictions:**
- Civilian duties performed in civilian clothing
- No operational command authority in retiree status
- Uniform wear limited to official functions and authorized occasions
- Retirees subject to UCMJ if recalled to active duty

## Chapter 2: Grooming Standards

### Section 1: General Grooming Standards

All military personnel must maintain grooming standards consistent with professional military appearance (PMA). Standards apply during all duty hours and while in uniform. Grooming standards also apply to retirees when wearing the uniform.

#### Article 2101: Hair Standards - Male Personnel

**Hair Length and Style:**
- Hair must be clean, neatly combed, and well-groomed at all times
- Hair color must be natural (no dyed colors other than black, brown, blonde, or red)
- Hair must not extend below the collar in the back
- Hair must not extend beyond the ear lobes on the sides
- Hair must not extend below the eyebrows in front
- Bulk of hair when combed should present a tapered appearance
- Sideburns may be present but must be straight-edged and end above the lobe

**Authorized Styles:**
- Standard haircut with appropriate taper
- Flat top style (must maintain above-mentioned length requirements)
- Shaved head (completely bald) authorized
- Fade haircut authorized
- Undercut style authorized (if maintains length requirements)

**Unauthorized Styles:**
- Cornrows not authorized for male personnel
- Dreadlocks not authorized
- Braids not authorized (except authorized military braid allowances)
- Mohawks or extreme styles not authorized
- Designs cut into hair not authorized
- Hair extensions not authorized for males

#### Article 2102: Hair Standards - Female Personnel

**Hair Length and Wear Options:**
- Hair must be clean, neatly styled, and well-groomed at all times
- Hair may be worn both up and down
- When hair is worn down in its natural state, it must not extend more than one-quarter inch below the lower edge of the collar
- Hair worn up allows greater length flexibility while maintaining professional appearance
- Large volume or extreme styles not appropriate even if within length guidelines

**Authorized Hair Styles (Worn Up):**
- Single bun (centered at base of skull)
- Double bun (two buns at equal height)
- Ponytail (single or double)
- French braid (single or double)
- Dutch braid (single or double) - AUTHORIZED as of March 27, 2024
- Twisted knots
- All-aft braids (multiple braids together)
- Combination of above styles
- Hair wraps (using matching colored material)

**Hair Color:**
- Natural colors authorized: Black, brown, blonde, red
- Dyed hair acceptable if remains natural-looking color
- Unusual colors (blue, purple, green, pink, etc.) not authorized
- Hair dye must be applied so roots are not visible or are similar color

**Hair Accessories and Ornaments:**
- Hair clips/barrettes must be professional and conservative
- Authorized colors: Black, brown, or matching hair color
- Small metal clips authorized if conservative
- No large decorative clips with excessive ornamentation
- Hair bands must match hair color or be conservative
- Pearls and conservative jewelry clips authorized

**False Hair and Extensions:**
- Hair extensions authorized if:
  - Match natural hair color
  - Maintain overall professional military appearance
  - Do not create distracting bulk or volume
  - Properly secured and neatly maintained
- Wigs authorized for medical reasons (e.g., alopecia treatment)
- Temporary hair extensions for special occasions acceptable if authorized by command

### Section 2: Facial Hair Standards

#### Article 2201: Facial Hair - Male Personnel

**General Policy:**
- Cleanly shaven face is the Navy standard and default expectation
- Beards authorized as of August 23, 2021 under specific guidelines
- Mustaches authorized under grooming standards
- Goatees permitted under specific criteria
- Religious grooming accommodations evaluated on case-by-case basis

**Beard Standards:**
- Must be neatly trimmed at all times
- Maximum length: One-quarter inch (1/4") from the face
- Beard must be even and present a neat, professional appearance
- Must not extend beyond the collar line (rear)
- Must not extend beyond the commissure of the mouth (sides)
- Color: May be natural color different from head hair
- Beard shaping: Even outline without extreme styles
- Mustache portion of beard must not be separately distinguished

**Mustache Standards:**
- May be worn alone or as part of beard
- Must be neatly trimmed at all times
- Must not extend beyond the corners of the mouth
- Must be symmetric and evenly shaped
- Handlebar style not authorized (tips may not curl upward)
- Color should blend with overall appearance

**Goatee Standards:**
- Goatees permitted if meet beard/mustache standards
- Must be neatly maintained
- Maximum length: One-quarter inch
- Must not extend below the chin in any direction
- Must maintain even, professional appearance
- Combination with sideburns not authorized

**Sideburn Standards:**
- May be worn but must end at ear lobe level
- Must be straight-edged
- Must not be thick or overly prominent
- Must be evenly shaped on both sides

**Transition from Bearded to Shaven:**
- Sailors initiating beard growth must maintain grooming standards throughout transition
- Beard growth should be neat and controlled
- No excuse for unkempt appearance during growth phase
- Facial hair grooming products acceptable for maintaining standards

#### Article 2202: Facial Hair - Female Personnel

**Policy:**
- Facial hair should be removed or minimal
- If left unshaved for religious or medical reasons, must be presented in neat appearance
- Minimal facial hair acceptable with proper maintenance
- Professional appearance standards apply

#### Article 2203: Religious Grooming Accommodations

**Beard Requests Based on Religious Belief:**
- Sailors may request religious accommodation for beard growth
- Request submitted to commanding officer
- Request evaluated under religious accommodation policy
- Accommodation weighed against military necessity and readiness
- If approved, sailor must maintain grooming standards (neatness, length, appearance)

**Documentation:**
- Written request to commanding officer
- Religious leader documentation of religious requirement (if applicable)
- Medical documentation (if medical reasons stated)
- Approval documented in personnel record

### Section 3: Cosmetics, Makeup, and Personal Grooming (Female Personnel)

#### Article 2301: Cosmetics Standards (UPDATED March 27, 2024)

**General Policy:**
- Cosmetics are optional but, if worn, must be applied conservatively
- Exaggerated or theatrical cosmetics not authorized
- Colors must be complementary to skin tone
- Appearance must be natural and professional
- Cosmetics must enhance rather than distract

**Lipstick:**
- Conservative colors authorized: Red, pink, coral, nude, tan, burgundy
- Colors must present complementary appearance
- Bright or neon colors not authorized
- Lip liner should match lipstick color
- Gloss acceptable if not overly shiny or distracting

**Eye Makeup:**
- Eye shadow in conservative colors (neutral tones, soft blues, silvers)
- Eyeliner authorized in black or brown
- Mascara authorized in black or brown
- Color application should enhance eye area without appearing theatrical
- Dramatic or glittery eye makeup not authorized

**False Eyelashes (UPDATED March 27, 2024):**
- False eyelashes now authorized with specific criteria (as of March 27, 2024)
- Must project a natural appearance
- Maximum length: 14 millimeters (measured from eyelid to tip of lash)
- Color: Must match natural eyelash color (black or brown)
- Eyelash extensions authorized under same guidelines
- Extensions must not hinder wear of protective eyewear (safety glasses, etc.)
- Any eyelashes preventing proper equipment use not authorized

**Nail Polish and Manicures:**

*Authorized Colors:*
- Red (all shades)
- Pink (pale to hot pink)
- Coral
- Nude (tan, beige)
- Clear
- French manicure with conservative colors

*Standards:*
- Colors must be conservative and professional
- One color on all nails (multi-colored nails not authorized)
- High-gloss finishes acceptable if professional
- Matte finishes acceptable
- All nails should be uniform in appearance

*Nail Length and Design:*
- Nails must not be excessively long (should not interfere with duties)
- No elaborate designs or embellishments authorized
- Nail art with patterns, gems, or decorations not authorized
- Simple striping acceptable if subtle
- Professional appearance standard must be met
- No detrimental effect on work performance or safety

#### Article 2302: Foundation, Concealer, and Base Makeup

**Standards:**
- Foundation must match skin tone
- Concealer should blend with foundation
- Base makeup should appear natural, not heavy or obvious
- Blending and application essential for professional appearance
- Overuse of powder not authorized (cakey appearance not professional)

#### Article 2303: Tanning and Spray Tans

**Policy:**
- Tanning for cosmetic purposes is personal choice
- Sun-tanning in uniform not appropriate
- Spray tans authorized if applied evenly and professionally
- Tanning results must not appear artificial or streaky
- Extreme tanning changes not consistent with professional appearance

### Section 4: Hair Removal and Body Grooming

#### Article 2401: Female Hair Removal

**Standards:**
- Underarm hair should be removed
- Leg hair removal is personal choice but commonly performed
- Facial hair above lip line typically removed
- Professional appearance standards guide expectations
- No specific mandate on body hair removal beyond professional appearance requirement

#### Article 2402: Male Body Grooming

**Standards:**
- Chest hair, arm hair, and leg hair are natural and acceptable
- Excessive body hair should not distract from uniform appearance
- Personal grooming choices permitted as long as professional appearance maintained
- No requirement for body hair removal

### Section 5: Eyeglasses and Sunglasses

#### Article 2501: Eyeglasses Standards

**Authorization:**
- Prescription eyeglasses authorized for wear in uniform
- Non-prescription eyeglasses (clear lenses) authorized if worn for functional reasons
- Reading glasses authorized as needed
- Eyeglasses must meet conservative appearance standards

**Frame Standards:**
- Conservative styles authorized
- Approved frame colors: Black, brown, tortoiseshell, gunmetal, silver, white gold
- Frame materials: Plastic or metal frames acceptable
- Oversized frames not authorized if distracting
- Designer frames acceptable if conservative style

**Lens Standards:**
- Clear lenses authorized (prescription or non-prescription)
- Lightly tinted lenses (amber or gray) authorized
- Photochromic lenses (change tint in sunlight) authorized
- Mirrored lenses not authorized
- Overly dark lenses not authorized with regular uniform
- Blue, pink, or unusual colored lenses not authorized
- Transition lenses in conservative tints acceptable

**Wear Standards:**
- Eyeglasses must fit securely on face
- Eyeglasses should not slip or require constant adjustment
- Eyeglasses must not obstruct vision or interfere with uniform wear
- Temple arms should not extend beyond head width

#### Article 2502: Sunglasses Standards

**Authorization:**
- Sunglasses authorized for wear during outdoor activities and outdoor duty
- Must be removed when entering buildings or indoors
- Not authorized when rendering honors or in formation (unless on duty requiring sun protection)
- Must be worn correctly (not pushed up on head or hanging on shirt)

**Style and Appearance:**
- Conservative frame styles authorized
- Oversized or unusual styles not authorized
- Frame colors: Black, dark brown, gunmetal, silver, white/pearl
- Lens colors: Amber, gray, brown, or blue tint
- Mirrored or reflective lenses not authorized
- Polarized lenses authorized
- Gradient lenses authorized if conservative

**Brand and Designer Sunglasses:**
- Name-brand sunglasses acceptable if meet color/style standards
- Designer logos/markings acceptable if minimal and not overly prominent
- Retro styles acceptable if conservative in appearance
- Aviator style sunglasses professional and appropriate

**Issue and Type:**
- Navy Issue sunglasses acceptable
- Commercially purchased sunglasses acceptable if meet standards
- Corrective prescription sunglasses authorized
- Sport-style sunglasses acceptable if conservative

### Section 6: Jewelry and Personal Accessories

#### Article 2601: Watches and Wristwear

**Watches:**
- One wristwatch authorized per sailor
- Conservative appearance required
- Band: Leather, metal, or fabric in black, dark blue, brown, silver, or gold
- Face: Conservative dial; visible numbers or minimalist style
- No digital watches with excessive features (though digital authorized if conservative)
- Sports watches acceptable if conservative in appearance
- Smartwatches authorized if meeting appearance standards

**Watch Band Colors:**
- Black leather: Most professional
- Brown leather: Acceptable
- Navy blue: Acceptable
- Silver metal: Professional
- Gold metal: Professional
- White gold: Acceptable
- Two-tone metal: Acceptable if conservative
- Fabric bands: Navy, black, or conservative colors

**Bracelets:**
- Female sailors: One bracelet authorized (optional)
- Male sailors: No bracelets authorized (except medical alert)
- Bracelet materials: Metal or leather
- Colors: Gold, silver, white gold, black, brown, navy
- Medical alert bracelets authorized for all
- Fitness tracker bracelets acceptable if conservative
- Paracord bracelets not authorized
- Beaded or ornamental bracelets not authorized

#### Article 2602: Earrings Standards

**Male Sailors (UPDATED August 23, 2021 and July 30, 2025):**
- One earring per ear authorized (as of August 23, 2021)
- Earrings revised again July 30, 2025
- Style: Studs or small hoops only
- Maximum diameter: 6 millimeters
- Material: Silver, white gold, pearl, or diamond
- Color: Silver, gold, pearl, or diamond
- No dangling, distracting, or excessively decorative earrings
- Religious earrings (bindi, sacred beads) accommodated when authorized
- Earrings must not interfere with uniform or hearing protection

**Female Sailors:**
- Two earrings per ear authorized (optional)
- Studs or small hoop styles only
- Hoops and studs must be matching pairs in same ear
- No dangling or excessively large earrings
- Maximum size: One-half inch in diameter
- Material: Gold, silver, pearl, or diamonds
- Colors: Gold, silver, pearl, or diamonds
- No unusual colors or distracting styles
- Earrings must not interfere with uniform or hearing protection
- No gauge earrings (stretched ear lobes)

#### Article 2603: Necklaces and Pendants

**Policy:**
- Necklaces not authorized to be worn visible outside uniform
- Necklaces must remain completely under uniform top
- Religious medallions authorized for wear under uniform (not visible)
- Religious necklaces permitted if worn under uniform
- Chain material: Gold, silver, or conservative metal (not ornate or decorative)
- Pendant: Small and conservative (less than 1 inch dimension)

**Religious and Medical Exceptions:**
- Religious medallions worn under uniform for faith purposes
- Medical alert necklaces authorized (worn under uniform if possible)
- Military dog tags worn per regulation (under uniform shirt)

### Section 7: Tattoos and Body Modifications

#### Article 2701: Tattoos

**Policy:**
- Tattoos must not be offensive, obscene, or represent extremist ideologies
- Visible tattoos must maintain professional military appearance
- Vulgar or obscene language/imagery not authorized on body
- Gang-related, racist, or discriminatory imagery not authorized
- Extremist group symbols not authorized

**Visible Tattoo Standards:**
- If visible when in uniform, must be professional in appearance
- Offensive tattoos must be covered or removed
- Command may require covering of questionable tattoos
- Large offensive tattoos may be cause for administrative action

**Professional Review:**
- Commanding officer may require review of visible tattoos
- Sailor may be asked to cover tattoo or provide explanation
- If tattoo represents extremist organization or ideology, subject to separation action
- Medical removal or covering authorized at sailor's expense

#### Article 2702: Body Piercing

**Policy:**
- Only ear piercings authorized for wear (per earring standards)
- No other facial piercings authorized (nose, eyebrow, tongue, lip)
- No body piercings authorized for wear in uniform
- Piercing holes other than ears must be left unpierced or covered with makeup
- Gauged ear lobes not authorized

**Medical Alert Devices:**
- Medical alert bracelets and necklaces authorized (worn under uniform if possible)
- Diabetes alert devices authorized
- Allergy alert devices authorized
- MedicAlert identification authorized

#### Article 2703: Intentional Skin Modifications (Branding, Scarification)

**Policy:**
- Body branding not authorized unless for medical reasons (e.g., surgical scars)
- Cosmetic scarification (decorative cutting) not authorized
- Keloid formation from piercings should be treated medically
- Medical skin treatments (tattoo removal, scar revision) authorized

### Section 8: Nail Standards and Grooming

#### Article 2801: Nail Length and Maintenance

**Male Personnel:**
- Nails must be clean and neatly manicured
- Natural nail color standard
- No nail polish authorized for male sailors
- Nails must not be excessively long
- Nails must not interfere with equipment operation or duties
- Jagged or broken nails must be filed smooth

**Female Personnel:**
- Nails should be neatly manicured
- Nail length should not interfere with duties or equipment use
- Nails must not be so long as to create safety hazard
- Professional appearance maintained
- See Article 2301 for nail polish standards

#### Article 2802: Nail Health and Sanitation

- Regular cleaning of nails and under-nail area required
- Nail grooming tools must be clean
- Artificial nails must be applied hygienically
- Infection or fungus requiring medical treatment
- Damaged or infected nails should be treated medically

### Section 9: Retiree Grooming Standards

#### Article 2901: Retirees in Uniform

**Policy:**
- Grooming standards apply when wearing Navy uniform
- Retirees not on active duty may have relaxed standards
- However, professional military appearance still expected
- Standards must still maintain dignity of uniform
- Retirees should present positive image of Navy

**Application:**
- Hair and facial hair standards apply
- Eyeglass and sunglasses standards apply
- Jewelry standards apply
- Earring standards apply
- Professional appearance in uniform required

#### Article 2902: Retirees in Civilian Clothes

**Policy:**
- No grooming standards apply when in civilian clothing
- Personal choice governs appearance in civilian clothes
- Navy grooming standards only apply in uniform
- Retirees may wear beards, dyed hair, unusual jewelry in civilian status

## Chapter 3: Uniform Components

### Section 1: Overview and General Requirements

#### Article 3101: Purpose and Scope

Naval uniforms consist of various components that sailors mix and match based on occasion for wear, season, rank, gender, and duty assignment. This chapter details each component, proper composition, and authorized wear.

**Uniform Categories:**
1. Dress Uniforms (Dinner Dress, Full Dress)
2. Service Dress Uniforms (Service Dress Blue)
3. Working Uniforms (Navy Working Uniform Types I, II, III)
4. Physical Training Uniform (PTU)
5. Flight Clothing (flight suits, flight jackets)

- Title: Chief [Rating] (e.g., Chief Petty Officer, Machinery Repairman [CPO(MR)])

#### E8 - Senior Chief Petty Officer (SCPO)
- Anchor with Navy star and eagle, with rocker arc beneath
- Rating specialty badge on sleeve
- Multiple chevrons and specialty marks
- Title: Senior Chief [Rating] (e.g., Senior Chief Petty Officer, Machinery Repairman [SCPO(MR)])

#### E9 - Master Chief Petty Officer (MCPO)
- Anchor with Navy star, eagle, and higher rocker arc
- Rating specialty badge
- Full set of chevrons displayed
- Title: Master Chief [Rating] or Master Chief Petty Officer [Rate abbreviation]

#### E9 Special Assignments
- Senior Enlisted Advisor to Chairman Joint Chiefs of Staff (SEAC)
- Fleet/Force Master Chief Petty Officer
- Command Master Chief Petty Officer
- Unique insignia or badges for special positions

### Enlisted Rates and Insignia (E4-E6)

**E4 - Petty Officer Third Class (PO3)**
- Single chevron and anchor
- Rate specialty badge above insignia
- Title: Petty Officer Third Class [Rate] (PO3[Rate abbreviation])

**E5 - Petty Officer Second Class (PO2)**
- Two chevrons and anchor
- Rate specialty badge
- Multiple specialty marks for advanced training
- Title: Petty Officer Second Class [Rate] (PO2[Rate abbreviation])

**E6 - Petty Officer First Class (PO1)**
- Three chevrons and anchor
- Rate specialty badge
- Full specialty marking display
- Title: Petty Officer First Class [Rate] (PO1[Rate abbreviation])

**E1-E3 - Recruit Through Petty Officer Apprentice**
- Stripes or single anchor (no chevrons)
- No rate specialty badge
- Training insignia if applicable
- Titles: Seaman Recruit (SR), Seaman Apprentice (SA), Seaman (SN)

### Special Qualifications and Insignia

**Dolphins - Submarine Qualification**
- Worn on breast
- Silver dolphins straddling a submarine
- Officers and enlisted qualified personnel
- Indicates completion of submarine warfare qualification

**Aircrew Wings - Flight Officer**
- Worn on breast
- Designates authorized flight officer personnel
- Different styles for different aircraft communities
- Requirements for earning depend on billet

**Command-at-Sea Insignia**
- Worn on upper sleeve or shoulder board
- Indicates command-at-sea assignment
- Officers and senior enlisted

**Command Ashore/Project Manager Insignia**
- Worn on uniform components
- Indicates shore command or project management assignment
- Officer-only designation

**Aerial Vehicle Pilot Qualification**
- Designated insignia worn on uniform
- Indicates pilot qualification for unmanned or aerial vehicles
- Added in recent regulations

**Surface Chaplain Qualification**
- Special insignia for religious professional officers
- Worn with chaplain corps insignia
- Indicates surface ship assignment and qualification

**Senior Enlisted Advisor, Chairman Joint Chief of Staff (SEAC)**
- Unique insignia added in 2023
- Worn by senior enlisted advisor to CJCS
- Highest enlisted position in Department of Defense

### Insignia Placement Standards

**Shoulder Board Placement (Officers)**
- Centered on shoulder board
- Proper alignment with uniform jacket seam
- No tilting or misalignment
- Secured with proper fasteners

**Sleeve Placement (Enlisted Rating Badges)**
- Centered on upper arm
- Approximately 2-3 inches from top of sleeve seam
- Properly sewn and aligned
- No slanting or misalignment

**Breast Insignia Placement**
- Centered on chest
- Proper spacing from name tape or other components
- Aligned with uniform guidelines
- No overlapping other insignia

## Chapter 7: Civilian Clothing

### General Standards for Civilian Attire

When not wearing naval uniforms, personnel are expected to maintain professional appearance through civilian clothing choices.

### Male Civilian Clothing Standards

#### Office and Professional Settings
- Business suit or sports coat and trousers
- Dress shirt (white, light blue, or neutral colors)
- Conservative tie or bowtie
- Closed-toe dress shoes (black, brown, or dark colors)
- Belt coordinating with shoes

#### Casual Professional
- Golf shirt or casual button-up shirt
- Khaki or casual trousers
- Closed-toe casual shoes (loafers, boat shoes)
- No athletic wear unless at fitness facilities

#### Casual Occasions
- Jeans or casual trousers (neat and clean)
- Casual shirt or golf shirt
- Casual shoes or sneakers (clean condition)
- No excessively worn or damaged clothing

#### Authorized Earrings (Male - Updated July 2025)
- One earring per ear authorized in civilian clothing
- Studs or small hoops acceptable
- Conservative appearance required
- Must not exceed 6mm in diameter
- Material: Silver, white gold, pearl, or diamond
- This updated policy allows for personal expression while maintaining military bearing

### Female Civilian Clothing Standards

#### Office and Professional Settings
- Business suit, blazer with trousers or skirt, or professional dress
- Professional blouse or dress shirt
- Closed-toe dress shoes (pumps, flats, loafers)
- Professional accessories (bag, belt)

#### Casual Professional
- Slacks or professional capris
- Casual blouse or golf shirt
- Closed-toe shoes (loafers, flats, or casual pumps)
- Minimal jewelry and accessories

#### Casual Occasions
- Jeans or casual pants (neat and clean)
- Casual shirt or blouse
- Casual shoes or clean sneakers
- No excessively worn or damaged clothing

### General Civilian Clothing Prohibitions

#### Unacceptable Civilian Attire
- Swimwear (except at beaches or pools)
- Excessively torn or damaged clothing
- Transparent clothing or clothing revealing intimate areas
- Clothing with offensive language or graphics
- Clothing promoting illegal activities or hate groups
- Pajamas or house clothing worn in public

#### Seasonally Inappropriate Clothing
- Heavy winter coats in summer
- Sleeveless in professional settings during cool weather
- Excessive layering in warm weather

### Special Situations and Exceptions

#### Navy Flight Demonstration Squadron (Blue Angels)
- Updated civilian clothing requirements per March 2024 changes
- Formal attire required for public appearances
- Professional appearance standards strictly enforced
- Blue Angels personnel adhere to unique standards

#### Physical Fitness and Athletic Settings
- Athletic wear authorized at fitness facilities
- T-shirts, shorts, and athletic shoes acceptable
- Can be worn to/from fitness facilities
- PTU uniform may substitute for athletic wear

#### Off-Base Recreation and Leisure
- Casual clothing acceptable
- Professional appearance still expected
- No offensive or inappropriate attire
- Commands may provide specific guidance

#### Religious and Cultural Events
- Accommodations made for religious dress
- Cultural attire honored when not conflicting with military standards
- Professional appearance maintained
- Religious medallions worn under uniform acceptable

### Headwear in Civilian Clothing

- Baseball caps and casual hats authorized
- Professional appearance maintained
- Inappropriate messages or graphics prohibited
- Religious head coverings accommodated

### Footwear in Civilian Clothing

- Closed-toe shoes preferred for professional settings
- Casual shoes acceptable for non-professional occasions
- Sandals acceptable in casual recreational settings
- No bedroom slippers in public areas
- Shoes must be clean and maintained

## Awards and Medals

### Navy Awards Precedence Chart

Personnel must wear awards and medals in proper order of precedence. The official Navy Awards Precedence Chart provides the authoritative sequence for ribbon placement.

**Key Precedence Categories (in order):**

1. Personal Decorations (medals earned for valor or meritorious service)
2. Campaign and Service Medals (earned for participation in military campaigns or service)
3. Non-Combatant Service Medals
4. Naval Reserve Meritorious Service Medal
5. Navy and Marine Corps Commendation Medals
6. Navy and Marine Corps Achievement Medals
7. Coast Guard medals (if applicable for Navy Joint assignments)
8. Meritorious Unit Commendation
9. Navy Unit Commendation
10. Navy Expeditionary Medal
11. Campaign Medals (various theaters and operations)
12. Service Medals and Ribbons
13. Training and Qualification Ribbons

### Medals Available

**Personal Decorations Include:**
- Navy Medal of Honor - Highest honor; extremely rare
- Navy Cross - Second-highest award for gallantry
- Distinguished Service Medal - Exceptional service in position
- Navy and Marine Corps Medal - Heroism not involving enemy
- Silver Star - Gallantry in action
- Legion of Merit - Exceptional service
- Meritorious Service Medal - Outstanding non-combatant service
- Air Medal - Heroism or meritorious service in aerial flight
- Navy and Marine Corps Commendation Medal - Meritorious service or heroism
- Navy and Marine Corps Achievement Medal - Outstanding achievement or service
- Navy Letter of Commendation Ribbon - Official commendation recognition

**Campaign Medals Include:**
- Global War on Terrorism Expeditionary Medal
- Armed Forces Expeditionary Medal
- Korean Service Medal (if applicable)
- Vietnam Service Medal (if applicable)
- Southwest Asia Service Medal
- Various other campaign medals for specific operations

**Service Medals and Ribbons Include:**
- Navy and Marine Corps Overseas Service Ribbon
- Naval Reserve Meritorious Service Medal
- Armed Forces Reserve Medal
- Navy Arctic Service Ribbon
- Humanitarian Service Medal
- Navy and Marine Corps Parachutist Insignia Ribbon
- Navy Diver Insignia Ribbon
- Various other service recognition ribbons

**Marksmanship Medals and Ribbons:**
- Navy Rifle Marksmanship Medal
- Navy Pistol Marksmanship Medal
- Navy Rifle Marksmanship Ribbon
- Navy Pistol Marksmanship Ribbon

### Ribbon Placement and Wear

**Ribbon Rack Assembly:**
- Ribbons mounted on horizontal bar

**Transitioning to Full Medal Wear:**
- Full medals worn at formal events
- Ribbons worn during daily uniform wear
- Some occasions specify medal or ribbon requirement
- Command guidance provided for specific events

**Hair Standards:**
- ✓ Clean and neatly combed/styled
- ✓ Natural colors (black, brown, blonde, red authorized)
- ✓ Males: Below collar, beyond ears, above eyebrows
- ✓ Females: 1/4 inch below collar when in natural state
- ✗ Unauthorized styles: Cornrows (male)
- ✓ Authorized styles (female): Buns, ponytails, braids, twists

- ✓ Clean-shaven standard for males
- ✓ Beards: 1/4 inch max, neatly trimmed
- ✓ Mustaches: Neatly trimmed, not beyond mouth corners
- ✓ Goatees: 1/4 inch max, neat appearance
- ✗ Excessive facial hair not permitted

**Makeup and Cosmetics:**
- ✓ Optional for female personnel
- ✓ Conservative colors complementary to skin tone
- ✓ Nail polish: Red, pink, coral, nude, clear, tan
- ✓ False eyelashes: Neat appearance, natural looking
- ✗ Theatrical or excessive makeup not authorized

- ✓ Male: One earring per ear (6mm max, studs/hoops)
